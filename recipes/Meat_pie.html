Meat Pie


Back Home

thickened filling of ground meat, onion and aromatics. British/Australian versions are usually baked in a double crust or topped with puff pastry; African, Caribbean and southern-U.S. versions are often half-moon hand pies that are fried. All versions freeze well and re-heat in an oven or air-fryer.

  • Make the filling
    a. Brown 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or 50 % beef + 50 % pork) in a wide pan.
    b. Add 1 small diced onion, 1 diced carrot or bell pepper, 2 cloves garlic, plus 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper, ½ tsp thyme, 1 tsp Worcestershire, optional pinch chilli.
    c. When veg are soft, sprinkle in 2 Tbsp flour; stir 1 min.
    d. Add ¾ cup (180 ml) stock or milk; simmer 2 min until thick like chili.
    e. Cool completely (speed-up: spread on a plate & refrigerate 20 min).
  • Choose your pastry path
    A. Easy baked double-crust → store-bought short-crust or refrigerated pie dough.
    B. Puff-topped pot pies → 1 sheet thawed puff pastry.
    C. Fried hand pies → homemade dough (4 c flour + 2 tsp baking powder + 1 tsp salt + 4 oz cold butter + 2 eggs + ~¾ c milk; knead, chill 30 min).
  • Heat control
    Baked pies: oven 400 °F / 200 °C now.
    Fried pies: pour 2 in / 5 cm oil into a pot and bring to 350 °F / 175 °C later.
  • Line or cut
    Big pie: lay bottom crust into 9-in dish.
    Hand pies: roll dough ⅛ in thick, cut 4-5 in circles (bowl or cutter).
  • Fill
    Big pie: spoon cold filling in, level, leave ½ in rim.
    Hand pies: place 2 Tbsp filling on one half of each circle.
  • Seal Big pie: wet rim, add top crust, crimp, cut 3 slits.
    Hand pies: wet edges, fold, press, crimp with fork; chill 15 min (prevents blow-outs).
  • Finish
    Brush with beaten egg (gloss) or milk (matte). Sprinkle a pinch of salt.
  • Cook
    Baked: 400 °F, lower-middle rack, 30–40 min until deep golden and bubbling.
    Fried: 350 °F, 2–3 min per side until mahogany; drain on rack or paper.
  • Rest
    10 min for baked pies (filling sets), 2 min for fried.
  • Serve / store
    Eat hot with ketchup, gravy or hot sauce. Cool leftovers, refrigerate 4 days or freeze 3 months. Reheat 375 °F oven 12-15 min (air-fryer 6-7 min 180 °C) to keep crust crisp.
